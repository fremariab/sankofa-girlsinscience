In rural Ghana, brilliant young girls often face a visibility gap, where becoming a scientist or engineer feels distant. The Sankofa Girls in Science Foundation was created to bridge that gap.

What began as one determined yes in 2010 has grown into a 15-year movement, empowering more than 500 girls to dream big and turn those dreams into reality.

The Founder's Story

I started this foundation to honor my parents' legacy and give forward the mentorship I received, because no girl in science should walk alone.

Nana Beecham is an educator and scientist who returned to Breman Asikuma with one conviction: girls in rural Ghana deserve equal access to scientific futures.

Fifteen years later, that vision has grown into a registered foundation and a global network of mentors and partners.

Nana Beecham

Founder and Executive Director, SGiS

01 Why We Exist

The gap is real.
We are closing it.

In Ghana, science classes often show a 2:1 boy-to-girl ratio, and the gap widens in higher education and technical programs. This is not a talent gap. It is an opportunity gap.

SGiS closes that gap by connecting girls with real role models and practical support, proving that science is for every capable mind.

02 How We Started

One school.
One yes.

It began in 2010 at Breman Asikuma Senior High School. Founder Nana Beecham returned home to plant a seed of possibility for girls in science.

What started as a pilot became a movement. In 2025, SGiS was formally established as a licensed foundation with a global board from Ghana and the United States.

03 What We Believe

Believed in.
She becomes it.

When a girl is seen, believed in, and equipped, she does not only study science. She becomes a scientist, engineer, researcher, and leader.

Our motto remains clear: continue to dream, dream big, and make your dream a reality.

Our Mission

To empower girls to see themselves in science, stay in the field, and become leaders who clear the path for those who follow.

Sankofa guides our philosophy: honor the past, build the future, and move forward with purpose.

Our Journey

15 years of
transformation

2010
SGiS pilot launched at BASHS after Nana Beecham returned to Breman Asikuma.
2012
First mentees gained admission into science programs at Ghanaian universities.
2015
Girls in Science Club launched to grow peer mentorship and leadership.
2018
SGiS Science Library and Reading Room dedicated at BASHS.
2020
Program reached 300+ girls with soft-skills and leadership curriculum.
2024
500+ girls reached and alumni progressed into STEM-related higher education.
2025
SGiS formally established as a licensed nonprofit foundation.
