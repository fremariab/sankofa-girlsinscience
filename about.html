OUR STORY

From Roots to Wings: Transforming Lives Through Mentorship in STEM.

A Spark in 2010

In July 2010, telecom executive Nana Beecham returned home to Ghana, inspired by her experiences in the Fortune/U.S. State Department Women's Mentoring Partnership. While being mentored at Xerox in Rochester, New York, she met the Black Women's Leadership Council (BWLC).

The BWLC had a dream: to light a spark in African girls studying science. Nana’s answer was an immediate "Yes." She chose her hometown of Breman Asikuma as the starting point, specifically Breman Asikuma Senior High School (BASHS), to honor her parents' legacy and the gift of education that transformed their lives.

The Meaning of "Sankofa"

"Return and fetch it—take from the past what you need to build the future."

Our name reflects our mission: honoring our roots while reaching forward. We believe that mentorship is a bridge that honors the past, empowers the present, and shapes the future.

Our Mission

To serve as a catalyst to advance professional and personal development of girls within the STEM community.

The Challenge

In Ghana, the ratio of girls to boys in science drops from 2:1 in secondary school to 7:1 in university medical programs. We exist to break this cycle.

Our Impact

Over 15 years, we have impacted 500+ students, built a dedicated Science Library, and seen over 50% of our participants pursue higher education.

A LEGACY OF LEADERSHIP

SGiS was built on the foundation of the BWLC at Xerox, founded in 1986 by visionary women including Sylvia Chambers, Marlene Williams, and others.
