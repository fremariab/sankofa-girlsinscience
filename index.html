ABOUT US

In 2010, Nana Beecham returned to her hometown of Breman Asikuma with a single mission: to prove that a girl’s potential is not defined by her geography. What began as a one-off mentoring program with the Black Women’s Leadership Council (BWLC) at Xerox has blossomed into a movement that is 15 years old and counting!.

Our name comes from the Ghanaian proverb "Sankofa" which means "Return and fetch it". We believe in reaching back to our roots to empower the next generation. We are bridging the gap in Ghana’s STEM landscape—where the ratio of boys to girls in science can drop from 2:1 in high school to a staggering 7:1 in university.

As such, we have set out to not only promote the study of science but build the confidence for girls to lead it. Through global mentorship and hands-on resources, we ensure that when a girl rises, her entire community rises with her.

WHAT WE DO

01.

SGiS Mentoring & Career Counseling

Connect students with professional women in STEM to provide academic insights, electronic communication skills, and the confidence to set "big, hairy, audacious" goals.

02.

Science Clinics & Workshops

Intensive sessions designed to prepare graduating students for final exams and hands-on career guidance days that reach girls as young as 11.

03.

Resource Centers

We dedicated a fully equipped Science Library and Reading Room at Breman Asikuma Senior High School to provide up-to-date textbooks and learning materials and we are soon launching a tech lab for girls in the heart of Tema.
